## Wound care instructions

The wound should be kept **clean and dry for the next 24–48 hours**. After this period, it may be gently cleaned **once daily with soap and water in the shower**, patted dry with a clean towel, and covered with a clean dressing as required.

You should:
- Wash hands before and after touching the wound or changing dressings
- Change the dressing daily or as instructed
- Avoid scratching or picking at the wound or scab
- **Avoid soaking the wound or swimming** until it is fully healed

Some mild redness or a small amount of yellowish discharge may be normal during healing.

## Red flags / when to seek review

Seek urgent medical review if any of the following occur:
- Fever (temperature **>38°C**) or chills
- Increasing or severe pain, swelling, or worsening redness
- Redness spreading or streaking away from the wound
- Offensive odour or purulent (yellow, green, or grey) discharge
- Any new or worsening symptoms of concern
