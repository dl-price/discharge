# Emergency Department Discharge Advice
## Pneumonia (Chest Infection)

### Why you came to the Emergency Department
You were seen in the Emergency Department because you were diagnosed with **pneumonia**, also known as a chest infection. Pneumonia is an infection of the lung tissue, most commonly caused by bacteria or viruses. It can cause symptoms such as cough, fever, shortness of breath, chest pain, and fatigue.

Based on your assessment today (including your symptoms, observations, and any tests performed), it is safe for you to continue your recovery **at home**.

---

### Looking after yourself at home
Recovery from pneumonia can take **several weeks**, even after treatment has started. It is normal to feel tired during this time.

Please follow this advice:

- **Rest regularly**, but try gentle activity (such as short walks) as you start to feel better.
- **Drink plenty of fluids** to avoid dehydration.
- Eat small, nutritious meals if your appetite is reduced.
- Avoid smoking or vaping.
- Avoid alcohol while unwell and while taking antibiotics.
- Gradually return to normal activities as your energy improves.

### Worsening advice
While it is safe for you continue treatment at home, sometimes things will get worse despite treatment.

#### Call **000** immediately if you develop:
- Severe difficulty breathing or you are struggling to speak in full sentences
- Blue or grey lips or fingertips
- Sudden collapse or loss of consciousness
- Severe chest pain not relieved by rest
- Confusion or marked drowsiness

#### Return to the Emergency Department urgently if you have:
- Worsening shortness of breath
- Fever that is not improving after **48 hours** of treatment
- Shaking chills or rigors
- Coughing up increasing amounts of sputum, especially if bloody
- Increasing chest pain
- Vomiting or inability to keep fluids or medications down
- You feel generally worse rather than better
- Any new or concerning symptoms
